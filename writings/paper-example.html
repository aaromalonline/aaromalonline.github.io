Solving Physics Olympiad via Reinforcement Learning on Physics Simulators

April 2026 · Mihir Prabhudesai, Aryan Satpathy, Aaromal A.

Abstract: We show that physics simulators can serve as an alternative source of supervision for training LLMs.

Abstract

We design a domain-specific language (DSL) to structure the randomization of scene graphs, enabling controlled variation along physically meaningful axes while guaranteeing validity and diversity. Randomization is explicitly restricted to parameters that affect system dynamics.

Question Answer Pair Generation

Since our scenes are structured, we can write simple natural language descriptions for each atomic element. By combining these descriptions according to the scene graph structure, we can automatically generate diverse question–answer pairs without human annotation.

Results

Across the evaluated model family, improvements on synthetic tasks consistently translate into gains on IPhO Mechanics, and this relationship holds across model scale.
