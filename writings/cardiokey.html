CardioKey (ECG-ID): Biometric Identification via Cardiac Signatures

2026

CardioKey is an analog-to-digital biometric system that identifies individuals using the unique structural variations in their electrocardiogram (ECG) signals. Unlike traditional PPG sensors, ECG captures the underlying electrical conduction of the heart, which serves as a highly robust biometric identifier.

Analog Front-End Engineering

The system utilizes an extreme-precision analog front-end built around the AD620 Instrumentation Amplifier. To capture the millivolt-scale cardiac potentials amidst high common-mode noise, we engineered a multi-stage gain block:

  • Instrumentation Stage: Precise differential amplification using AD620 with a CMRR > 100 dB.
  • Gain Block: Provides approximately 1000x total amplification for stable ADC digitization.
  • Active Filtering: Integrated bandpass filtering (0.5 Hz to 40 Hz) to eliminate baseline wander and high-frequency powerline interference.

Signal Processing & Classifier Pipeline

Once digitized, the ECG signals are processed through a dimensionality reduction and classification pipeline. By extracting the P-QRS-T complex and applying Principal Component Analysis (PCA) followed by Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA), the system achieves a peak identification accuracy of 96% across evaluated test subjects.
